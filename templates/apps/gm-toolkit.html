Availability, Failure, and Commissioning

Difficult goods may carry markups of +25%, +50%, +100%, or more. Haggling reduces the marked-up price, but the merchant's minimum acceptable percentage is still based on the original price.

A failed AL check may delay another attempt, reveal a lesser or flawed alternative, attract unwanted attention, or provide a lead elsewhere. Rare gear may be commissioned when a buyer can find a craftsperson and the required materials.

Items of Power

World settings, overlap guidance, alternate slots, attunement variants, and Dark Magic Item corruption.

World Rules

Overlap or No Overlap?

Head: helmets, crowns, circlets, masks, hoods, cowls, headbands, and turbans overlap. Earrings, monocles, eye patches, hair pins, and jaw guards do not.

Body: most non-armor pieces can be worn over or under armor. An Item of Power shaped as armor overlaps and should use its armor-conflict and location fields. Armor+ pieces receive the normal Armor+ benefit.

Hands: gauntlets, vambraces, and pauldrons overlap. Bracers, gloves, and wristbands do not and can be worn together. Mark non-overlapping accessories on their item sheet.

Rings: one per finger or similar appendage rather than two total. Sheaths: four practical positions—left hip, right hip, back of hip, and backpack. GMs may allow sensible stacking while attunement remains the limiting rule.

Removing Attunement

When enabled, equipped Items of Power function without attunement checks or slot limits. This removes much of the incentive for non-magic characters to preserve Soul for attunement capacity.

Technology-Affected Attunement

Technological items can be marked as attuned against magical radiation. Each protected item consumes an attunement slot and is treated as stabilized against radiation-driven malfunction.

Dark Magic Items

Items created through souls, innocent blood, ritual sacrifice, Hell, corruption damage, consumed life or sanity, dark gods, or cosmic horrors are Dark Magic Items. Their gray-black flame or light-absorbing aura is visible to creatures that perceive magical auras.

  • Attuned: gain Sin equal to Corruption Value each dawn.
  • Carried but unattuned: gain 1 Sin each week.
  • Addiction: crossing the Sin Threshold triggers an addiction check as a Dark Magic spell with DT equal to Corruption Value.
